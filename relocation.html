Hekswerk Relocation Coaching

Systems-architecture approach to your international move.
Beta Program — Founding Cohort.

This is a description of a service I am building. I am currently in the active execution phase of my own DAFT-based relocation from Portland, Oregon to The Hague, with arrival planned for November 2026. After I land and settle in-country, this will become a formal advisory practice under Hekswerk.

Before that public launch, I am taking on a limited number of beta clients — at no cost or significant discount — in exchange for honest feedback, case-study rights, and a testimonial after the engagement. This document explains what the service will be, what you would get as a beta participant, and what I am explicitly not offering.

Who this is for

You are seriously considering relocating from the US to Europe. Maybe you have already picked a country; maybe you are weighing a few. You have realized this process touches dozens of interconnected systems — immigration law, housing markets, pet logistics, financial planning, healthcare enrollment, municipal bureaucracy, language, identity, family — and trying to research all of it simultaneously is overwhelming.

You don't need another blog post. You need someone who has mapped the system and can walk you through it without letting you drown.

This service is built especially for people whose specific lives don't fit standard relocation advice well:

  • Self-employed people, freelancers, and small-business owners considering DAFT or comparable self-employment routes
  • Neurodivergent planners who need bandwidth-aware pacing rather than a fixed schedule
  • Chronically ill folks for whom the move itself is a medical-logistical undertaking
  • LGBTQ+ people thinking about safety, healthcare access, and legal recognition in destination countries
  • Pet owners who refuse to put their animals in cargo and need non-flight alternatives mapped out
  • Couples and families coordinating two or more nervous systems through a multi-year transition

Who I am

I'm Levi Banks, founder of Hekswerk and a systems architect by trade. Hekswerk is my Dutch sole proprietorship (eenmanszaak) under the DAFT pathway, providing automation consulting, AI annotation work, and — increasingly — the structured relocation advisory described here.

I am currently in the active execution phase of my own DAFT relocation, with six years of advance research and planning behind me, professional immigration counsel engaged, and the road trip / transatlantic / ferry / corporate-housing logistics already booked. The relocation is happening November 2026.

My deepest expertise is the Netherlands and the DAFT pathway specifically. The methodology I use — system mapping, dependency tracking, critical-path planning, decision support — adapts to other European pathways, but for non-NL countries during the beta period, the engagement looks more like methodology-led co-research than country-specific deep advisory. I'll be transparent about that distinction with any beta client whose target country is not the Netherlands.

I'm not a lawyer, a financial advisor, or a therapist. I'm the person who sees the whole board and helps you move through it without losing your mind.

What you're paying for

You are not paying for my time. You are paying for the time you don't waste — the months of dead-end research you skip because I have already done it.

Every engagement includes:

  • Specific written deliverables you own and keep
  • A defined number of live touchpoints so you always know what is included
  • A clear endpoint — the engagement concludes; renewal is a separate decision

The Roadmap Package

The core offering. Beta participants receive this free or at significant discount. The Roadmap Package produces a comprehensive, personalized planning system for your specific relocation. It is not a generic checklist; it is a custom-built map of your move, with your constraints, timeline, household, and risk tolerance built in.

Written deliverables (yours to keep)

  • Relocation Systems MapEvery moving part of your move, how they connect, and where the leverage points are.
  • Critical Path TimelineWhat has to happen by when, with realistic ranges rather than fake-precise dates.
  • Dependency MapWhich decisions block which other decisions, so you never get blindsided by a sequencing problem.
  • Researched Provider ShortlistImmigration attorneys, domicile services, housing agents, pet transport specialists, with vetting notes on direct use, response quality, specialization, pricing transparency, and red flags. Inclusion is not a guarantee; you contract directly with providers.
  • Document Readiness ChecklistWhat you'll need, in what order, with the questions to ask your lawyer/accountant/provider when you hand them off (no legal interpretation or form preparation on my end).
  • Budget & Risk RegisterRealistic cost framework with category-level estimates, plus a register of the things most likely to go sideways and what to do about each.
  • First 90 Days Landing PlanMunicipal registration, BSN, banking, healthcare, building local routine. During beta, informed by my own arrival timeline; post-launch, by lived experience.

Live touchpoints

  • 90-minute deep-dive intake session at the start
  • Two 60-minute follow-up calls to refine the plan and answer questions
  • Asynchronous communication for clarifying questions throughout (business hours, 2-business-day response window)

Engagement length

Approximately 4 to 6 weeks from intake to delivery, depending on complexity and your own pacing.

The four phases

Phase 1 — Orientation & Assessment

We assess your full picture: timeline, budget, household and pet situation, risk tolerance, candidate visa pathway, and — critically — your capacity. How much bandwidth do you actually have for this process? What does your support system look like? We build the plan around you as a real person, not an abstract checklist.

Phase 2 — Legal & Administrative Orientation

I help you orient to the visa landscape, identify the right professionals to retain (immigration attorneys, domicile services, notaries, accountants), and prepare the questions to bring to those professionals. I'm explicit about the boundary: pathway orientation and attorney-prep support is mine; legal interpretation and document preparation is your lawyer's.

Phase 3 — Logistics Architecture

Transit planning, pet logistics, housing search strategy, temporary versus permanent accommodations, budgeting, contingency planning. The granular execution layer — turning the big vision into a sequence of bookable, actionable steps with clear dependencies.

Phase 4 — Landing & Integration

Municipal registration, BSN (Dutch national ID equivalent), healthcare enrollment, banking, building a local routine and support network. The first 90 days in-country are their own distinct challenge.

Implementation Support

For the active months of your move, after the Roadmap is delivered. Once you have your plan and you're executing, this is ongoing support for when questions arise, decisions need to be made, and things inevitably do not go according to plan.

  • Monthly retainer — three scheduled consultation hours per month, plus asynchronous support for brief questions. Response window: business hours, two business days. Fair-use applies (typically up to five threads per week). Urgent or time-sensitive sessions are billed separately. Usually the better fit, because relocation questions arrive in clusters.
  • Hourly — for clients who prefer flexibility or have lighter needs. Booked in 60- or 90-minute sessions.

À la carte

For past clients, or for people who only need a specific piece:

  • Focused 90-minute consultation on a single topic (e.g., "walk me through pet logistics")
  • Document readiness review — completeness, sequencing, and questions to bring to your lawyer or provider. Not legal interpretation or form preparation.
  • Urgent-decision session for time-sensitive situations

What's not in scope

Clarity about boundaries is what keeps this work clean for both of us:

  • Legal advice, legal interpretation, or form preparation — your immigration lawyer's job. I refer to ones I have worked with directly and trust.
  • Tax or financial planning — your accountant's job, ideally one who specializes in expat or cross-border tax. I refer.
  • Therapy or emotional processing — relocating internationally surfaces enormous feelings about identity, family, belonging, and loss. I can hold space briefly when something comes up in a session, but I will redirect you to a therapist for the deep work, and I think you should have one.
  • Acting as your realtor, vet, or travel agent — I recommend providers and help you evaluate options. I do not perform those services.
  • Country-specific deep expertise outside the Netherlands during the beta period — the methodology generalizes; my country-specific knowledge does not yet. For non-NL beta clients, the engagement is methodology-led co-research, and I will be explicit about what I know versus what I am researching alongside you.

Privacy & data handling

Hekswerk is a privacy-first practice. This is part of the offer architecture, not an afterthought. Relocation work involves handling sensitive personal data: passport scans, financial documents, medical and pet health records, addresses, immigration filings. Hekswerk operates under GDPR and treats client data with these principles:

  • Data minimization — I do not ask for passports, medical records, or financial documents unless strictly necessary for the work in front of us.
  • Secure intake — sensitive documents are handled through encrypted channels, never casually emailed.
  • Defined retention — sensitive documents are not retained after the engagement unless we have explicitly agreed otherwise in writing.
  • Client-owned deliverables — the deliverables I produce are yours; I keep an anonymized working copy for my own reference, never your raw data.
  • Transparent processing — a written privacy statement covers what I collect, why, how long it is kept, and your rights as a data subject.
