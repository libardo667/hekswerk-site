Relocation Systems Planning

For moves too specific for generic advice.

You are considering an international move, and the usual checklist does not quite fit: capacity, identity, household dynamics, medical continuity, safety, money, timing, or destination uncertainty are part of the system.

Start intake Compare engagements

The goal is not more research.

The goal is a planning system you can use: what has to happen, what depends on what, where specialists belong, and which decisions need your attention first.

Good fit

Built for people whose constraint matters.

The common thread is not a demographic label. It is a move that feels non-optional or high-stakes, paired with a standard relocation system that was not built for your actual life.

Capacity-limited

Neurodivergent, disabled, chronically ill, burned out, or otherwise unable to plan by brute force.

Identity-aware

LGBTQ+ people and households weighing safety, belonging, documentation, or support continuity.

Household-shaped

Partners, children, dependents, pets, family systems, or other people whose needs affect the move.

Still deciding

People comparing destinations, timelines, or pathways before committing to one version of the move.

Deliverables

Eight artifacts across the arc of the move.

The work is scoped around written planning deliverables, not undefined consulting time. Each artifact is built for use after the calls are over.

D-00

Intake & Situation Brief

Your canonical reference: where you are, where you are going, what is known, and what is hard.

D-01

Relocation Systems Map

A map of the systems involved in your move and the leverage points that change the plan.

D-02

Critical Path Timeline

What has to happen by when, with irreversible commitments called out clearly.

D-03

Dependency Map

What depends on what, so a slip in one area does not become invisible downstream.

D-04

Provider Shortlist

Attorneys, accountants, movers, housing providers, or other specialists screened for fit.

D-05

Document Readiness Checklist

The documents your move is likely to need, organized in the order you will need them.

D-06

Budget & Risk Register

Estimated costs, explicit risk holds, and the assumptions those numbers depend on.

D-07

First 90 Days Landing Plan

The first three months after arrival, sequenced so the first things happen first.

Boundaries

Hekswerk plans the move. Specialists do specialist work.

The scope discipline is part of the value. You get a planner who identifies handoffs, briefs providers well, and integrates their inputs into the larger system.

What Hekswerk does

Map systems and dependencies, sequence timelines, identify provider needs, prepare specialist handoffs, organize documents, and help you understand the whole board.

What Hekswerk does not do

Legal advice, immigration representation, tax advice, financial planning, therapy, realtor work, travel-agent work, or filing paperwork on your behalf.

Privacy
